Latest From The Dugout

Francisco Lindor underwent minor right elbow surgery on October 22, 2025, to remove damaged tissue and bone spurs. This follow-up procedure addresses ongoing discomfort and stiffness after a similar surgery in 2023. Lindor's recovery is expected to be quick, with minimal impact on his 2026 season availability, and he should be fully healed by spring training.

Edwin Díaz has opted out of his contract and is now a free agent, making him one of the top relievers available on the market. After a strong 2025 season, he is expected to seek a new deal, with some reports suggesting a potential return to the Mets or interest from other teams like the New York Yankees.

Juan Soto has been named one of the three finalists for the 2025 National League Most Valuable Player award, marking his first time earning the honor as a Met. Soto hit .263 with 43 home runs, 38 stolen bases, and a .921 OPS, becoming the first Met ever to record a 40-HR/30-SB season. The 2025 NL MVP winner will be announced on November 13.
